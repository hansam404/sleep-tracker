Why you feel unrefreshed

Screen light suppresses deep sleep
Blue light delays melatonin by up to 3 hours — your body enters sleep but skips restorative slow-wave stages, causing the "slept 8 hrs, feel terrible" pattern.
Irregular schedule disrupts your circadian rhythm
Variable sleep/wake times mean you're often sleeping at the wrong phase of your body clock, reducing deep and REM sleep.
Racing thoughts → cortisol → shallow sleep
Stress keeps cortisol elevated. High cortisol at bedtime reduces REM and deep sleep — you cycle in light sleep all night.

Evening wind-down routine

9:00 pm
Hard screen cutoff
Phone + laptop away. Switch to dim warm light. Non-negotiable for deep sleep.
9:15
Brain dump (5 min)
Write tomorrow's tasks + looping thoughts. Gets worries out of your head and onto paper.
9:30
Cool the room
Set AC/fan to 18–20°C. Body temperature drop triggers melatonin release.
9:45
Calm activity (30 min)
Physical book, gentle stretching, or light meditation. Low stimulation only.
10:15
4-7-8 breathing (3 min)
Inhale 4s → hold 7s → exhale 8s. Activates parasympathetic system, directly lowers cortisol.
10:30
Lights out — same time every night
Consistency matters more than duration. Same time ±20 min resets your circadian rhythm within ~2 weeks.
